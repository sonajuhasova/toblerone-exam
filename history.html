History of Toblerone

1867

HOW EVERYTHING BEGAN

The "non-cantonal" Jean Tobler is allowed to settle in Bern. He first works in the confectionery Kuentz in Marktgasse in Bern and when Kuentz falls ill, Tobler takes over the business.Since the location does not suit him, he set up his "Confiserie Spéciale" a few months later (1868) on the first floor of a new building on the Mittelstrasse in the Längssquartier. Initially, Jean Tobler only makes chocolate products in his shop from semi-finished products, which he sources from other well-known manufacturers.

1899

JEAN TOBLER LAUNCHES INDUSTRIAL CHOCOLATE PRODUCTION IN BERN

The demand for Tobler's chocolate products is so great that Jean Tobler opens his own chocolate factory. He and his sons founded the “Fabrique de Chocolat Berne, Tobler & Cie”

1908

THEODOR TOBLER AND EMIL BAUMANN INVENT THE TOBLERONE

Theodor Tobler (the son of Jean Tobler) and Emil Baumann (production manager and cousin of T. Tobler) invent a unique chocolate: TOBLERONE! With its special recipe, its triangular shape and its typical logo, TOBLERONE becomes the Swiss chocolate par excellence. "TOBLERONE" is a word consisting of the names "Tobler" and "Torrone" which refers to honey nougat with almonds in Italian.

1920

THE BERNER BEAR COMES ON THE PACK

The eagle is removed from the Toblerone packaging. It is replaced by the heraldic animal of Bern, the Bern Bear.

1970

THE MATTERHORN APPEARS ON THE PACK

The Matterhorn only appeared on the packaging of the Toblerone in 1970, not yet on the front, but on both sides.

1985

THE PRODUCTION IS MOVED TO BERN BRÜNNEN

As part of a major Swiss project, an entirely new chocolate factory is being built in west Bern, from where all produced Toblerone products are exported worldwide.

2000

THE BERNER BEAR AND THE MATTERHORN

The blue and white Tobler logo disappears, the Matterhorn now appears on the packaging in its place. By the way, there is something hidden in the Matterhorn. Can you see what it is?

2008

TOBLERONE CELEBRATES 100 YEARS

When we speak of “global brands” today, Toblerone is definitely one of them. Only a few chocolates have become synonymous with "Swiss quality chocolate". Fans of this Swiss specialty, whether they are in Tokyo, Rio, London or Berlin,can enjoy it in consistently high quality all over the world, with the certainty that it is a real piece of traditional Swiss chocolate.
